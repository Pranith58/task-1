“The specificities of the real estate market in Dubai”

All real estate markets have their specificities!​

In Europe, the closer to the city center, the more expensive is the square meter. In the US, Downtown is more reserved for businesses, while the most popular residential areas are generally located on the outskirts. In Morocco the city centers are often popular and crowded, due to the presence of the souks.

Dubai is a very specific market, with its own logic. The city, which is very recent, is organized around districts linked by high-speed motorway axes, allowing quick access from everywhere. ​

This new organization shuffles the cards and requires an analysis of the new parameters before purchase.

Our Specific Factors

The real estate developers

In the UAE, few manufacturers share the market. According to their reputation, the properties, despite their proximity, can have significant price difference

The community system

The development plans here are made by large real estate complexes, sometimes grouping together 10 towers and thousands of houses.

The high proportion of off-plan sales

More than elsewhere, the market attracts and almost half of the sales come from properties that will only be delivered in a few years.

Freehold/Leasehold

Depending on the districts, only the GCC population can be owners, while other zones the purchase is authorized for all
